About

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate in the Department of Geosciences at Princeton University. I recently finished my PhD in the School of Oceanography at the University of Washington, where I was advised by Professor Curtis Deutsch.

I am interested in the interconnections between climate, ocean oxygen and the spatial and temporal patterns of marine biodiversity and ecosystem function throughout Earth history and in the modern ocean. My approach is to develop and apply mathematical models of species physiology, marine ecosystems and ocean biogeochemistry with simulations of the Earth system. I strive to constrain and test these models with compilations of experimental and field data from a variety of rich climatic, biological, and paleontological sources. Such work is timely and critical, as anthropogenic climate warming risks oxygen depletion of the ocean, which among other environmental changes, threatens both global marine productivity and diverse animal life.

In my free time, enjoy exploring the California coastline in search of tasty waves to surf, wild mushrooms to forage, and concrete waves to skate.
